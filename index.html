Uttarakhand · Himalayan foothills · Banks of the Ganga

Rishikesh, reflected

A pilgrim town where suspension bridges sway over the Ganga, ashram bells mark the hour, and the Himalayas rise straight out of the riverbank. Every ghat, temple and trail on this page is a real place you can walk to.

01 · Landmarks & Bridges

The bridges that stitch the town together

Rishikesh is built around its river crossings — two great swaying bridges and the neighbourhoods that grew up on either bank.

Lakshman Jhula, the 450-foot iron suspension bridge, 1889
Landmark

Lakshman Jhula

The town's most photographed crossing — a narrow iron footbridge strung 70 feet above the Ganga, shared with cows, sadhus and troops of monkeys.

03 · Ashrams

Why they call it the yoga capital

Thirteen working ashrams, from century-old institutions to the studio where the Beatles once wrote songs.

Parmarth Niketan, one of Rishikesh's largest ashrams
Ashram

Parmarth Niketan

A sprawling riverside ashram with daily yoga halls and the flower-lined ghat where its Ganga Aarti is held each evening.

📍 View on Google Maps
Ashram

Sivananda Ashram

Founded by Swami Sivananda in 1936, one of the oldest and most influential seats of modern yoga teaching in India.

📍 View on Google Maps
Ashram

Maharishi Mahesh Yogi Ashram

Known simply as the Beatles Ashram — the abandoned retreat where the band studied meditation in 1968, now open as a gallery of graffiti art.

📍 View on Google Maps
Ashram

Art of Living Ashram

A large, modern campus on the hills above town offering breathing and meditation courses to visitors from around the world.

📍 View on Google Maps
Every evening, without fail

The Ganga Aarti

As the sun drops behind the hills, priests swing brass lamps of camphor flame along the riverbank while the crowd sings and floats marigold-lit diyas onto the current — the moment most visitors remember longest after they've left.

06 · Adventure & Outdoor

What put Rishikesh on the adrenaline map

India's white-water capital — rafting, jumping, flying and camping along a 16-mile stretch of the Ganga.

07 · Parks & Wildlife

Where the jungle starts

Rishikesh sits on the edge of Rajaji Tiger Reserve — elephants, leopards and over 300 bird species, a short drive from the ghats.

08 · Cafes & Riverside Restaurants

Where the yoga crowd eats

Fifteen riverside cafes built the Rishikesh way — low seating, sattvic menus, and a view of the water.
